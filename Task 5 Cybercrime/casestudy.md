# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive banking details or approving fraudulent transactions. It commonly occurs through fake phone calls, messages, or links pretending to be from banks or payment apps.

In a typical scenario, a fraudster calls a user claiming to be a bank official and informs them about an issue with their account. The attacker then sends a payment request or a malicious link and asks the user to approve it or enter their UPI PIN. Once the user approves the request, money is instantly transferred to the attacker’s account.

Students and elderly people are often targeted due to lack of awareness. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and risk of identity theft.

This case highlights the importance of being cautious while handling online transactions and verifying any suspicious requests before taking action.
